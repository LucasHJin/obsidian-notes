# Arrhenius Equation Chemistry

**What is it:** Used to find the value of $k$ (the rate constant) 
- Important because RoR depends on $k$ and concentration of reactants raised to a power ($[A]^n[B]^m$) 

**$k$ depends on 2 factors:** 
- Temperature
	- Will increase average energy of particles + number of collisions
		- Increases rate -> increases rate constant
- Catalyst
	- Alternative route with lower activation energy requirement
		- Increases rate -> increases rate constant

**Formula:** $k=A{{\text{e}}^{-\frac{{{E}_{\text{a}}}}{RT}}}$ 
- $k=$ rate constant (various units)
- $T=$ temperature (kelvin)
- $R=8.31\frac{J}{Kmol}=$ gas constant (joules per kelvin per mole)
- $E_a=$ activation energy (joules per mole) 
- $e=2.718=$ Euler's number (no units) 
- $A=$ the Arrhenius constant (various units)
	- **Arrhenius constant (pre-exponential factor):** Value that takes into account the frequency and orientation of collision (fulfills requirement for the proper orientation)
		- Generally considered constant with temperature and varies with different reactions
		- Same units as $k$ 
- Requirement for sufficient energy:
	- Fulfilled by ${{\text{e}}^{-\frac{{{E}_{\text{a}}}}{RT}}}$ 
		- Fraction of molecules with enough energy is dependent on $T$ and $E_a$ 
			- Not $R$ because it is constant

**Graphical representation of the Arrhenius equation (Arrhenius plot):** 
- $\ln k=-\frac{E_a}{RT}+\ln A$ 
	- **x-axis:** $\frac{1}{T}$ 
	- **y-axis:** $\ln k$ 
	- **slope:** $-\frac{E_a}{R}$ 
	- **y-intercept:** $\ln A$ 
- Can also calculate $A$ like this:
	- $A=\dfrac{k}{{{\text{e}}^{-\frac{{{E}_{\text{a}}}}{RT}}}}$ 
- Can also calculate $E_a$ given the $k$ for $2$ different values of $T$: 
	- $\ln \left( \dfrac{{{k}_{2}}}{{{k}_{1}}} \right)=-\dfrac{{{E}_{a}}}{R}\left( \dfrac{1}{{{T}_{2}}}-\dfrac{1}{{{T}_{1}}} \right)$ 
		- REMEMBER REVERSED
