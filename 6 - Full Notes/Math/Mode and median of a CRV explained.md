# Mode and median of a CRV explained

Review:
- [[Measures of central tendency explained]] 
	- Mode = most popular
	- Median = middle value

___
**Mode of a CRV:** 
- The value at which the probability density function (PDF) of the variable is at a maximum
- I.e. for $f\left(x \right) = \left\{ \begin{array}{c} \dfrac{4}{{25}}{x^2}{\rm{ }}&{\rm{if\;}}0 \leqslant x < 1\\ \dfrac{6}{{25}}x - \dfrac{2}{{25}}&{\rm{if}}\;{\rm{ }}1 \leqslant x < 2\\  - \dfrac{1}{5}{x^2} + x - \dfrac{4}{5}{\rm{ }}&{\rm{if\;}}2 \leqslant x \leqslant 4 \end{array} \right.$ 
	- ![[Screenshot 2024-08-14 at 11.34.10 AM.png|300]] Mode is at 2.5
- To find:
	- Take derivative and set to $0$ 
	- $f'(x)=0$ 

**Median of a CRV:** 
- The point where the area under the probability density curve reaches $\frac{1}{2}$ 
	- The value $m$ where $\int_{ - \infty }^m {f\left(x \right)\,{\rm{d}}x}  = \dfrac{1}{2}$ 
- For the above formula:
	- Find area of each part -> need 1st, second and part of 3rd
	- Median is $2.39$ 
- Can do the same for IQR, $Q_1$, $Q_3$, etc.
