**Expectation (mean) of a discrete random variable:** 
- $\bar{x}$ and $E(X)$ are very similar:
	- Use $E(X)$ for random variables, $\bar{x}$ 
	- Because experimental probability $\dfrac{{{f_i}}}{n}$ is an approximation of the theoretical probability $P(X=x_i)$.
		- $\bar x=\sum\limits_{i = 1}^k {{x_i}\dfrac{{{f_i}}}{n}}$ 
		- ${\rm{E}}\left(X \right) = \sum\limits_{i = 1}^k {{x_i}\;{\rm{P}}\left({X = {x_i}} \right)}$ 
	- Still just multiplying the chance of getting something by its value
- $\bar x=\mu=E(X)$ 

**Variance of a discrete random variable:** 
- Need new formula because random variables are based on the probability of outcomes occurring
	- Will use $Var(X)$ instead of $\sigma^2$ 
- $\operatorname{Var}(X)=\operatorname{E}\Big[(X-\mu)^2\Big]=\operatorname{E}(X^2)-\big[\operatorname{E}(X)\big]^2$ 
- $\displaystyle{\operatorname{Var}(X)= \sum(x-\mu)^2\operatorname{P}(X=x)= \sum x^2\operatorname{P}(X=x)-\mu^2 }$ 
- *To get standard devation* -> square root
