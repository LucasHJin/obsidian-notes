# m135 - Logical Operators and Algebra

Can combine logical operators with [[m135 - Conjunction and Disjunction|component statement variables]] to make complicated logical expressions
- Determine truth values for these logical expressions with [[m135 - Truth Tables|truth tables]] 
- I.e. ![[Screenshot 2025-08-06 at 3.14.57 PM.png]] 
**De Morgan’s Laws (DML):** For statement variables $A$ and $B$:
- $\neg (A\land B)\equiv (\neg A\land \neg B)$ 
- $\neg (A\vee B)\equiv (\neg A\vee \neg B)$ 

There exist rules for logical operators similar to the rules of algebra
- ![[Screenshot 2025-08-06 at 8.38.17 PM.png|300]] 
	- DML is similar to distributive

**Transitivity of logical equivalence:** can treat $\equiv$ as an equal sign for logical expressions
- I.e. $A_{1}\equiv A_{2}\equiv A_3$ → $A_1\equiv A_3$
