# Probability of related events explained

Remember:
- $n(A\cup B)=n(A)+n(B)-n(A\cap B)$ 
- $\rm{P}(A\cup B)=\rm{P}(A)+\rm{P}(B)-\rm{P}(A\cap B)$ 

**Conditional probability:** The probability of an event given that another event has occurred.
- Shrinks the total sample space -> either increases probability / renders 0
- **Probability of an event A occurring *given* B** $=\rm{P}(A\mid B)$ 
	- $\text{P}(A\mid B)=\frac{n(A\cap B)}{n(B)}=\frac{\text{P}(A\cap B)}{\text{P}(B)}$ 
- $\text{P}(B')=\text{P}(A\cap B')+\text{P}(A'\cap B')$ 

**Probability of independent events:** 
- $A$ and $B$ are independent if no impact on each other's occurences
	- $\rm{P}(B\mid A)=\rm{P}(B)$
	- $\rm{P}(A\mid B)=\rm{P}(A)$ 
	- From above, can derive:
		- $\rm{P}(A\cap B)=\rm{P}(A)\rm{P}(B)$
