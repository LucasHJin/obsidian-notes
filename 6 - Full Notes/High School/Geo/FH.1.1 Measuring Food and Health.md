# FH1 Measuring Food and Health

Third optional theme
- Economic development -> often accompanied by **dietary change** and an **epidemiological transition** in which diseases become less common and diseases of affluence become more common
	- Not apply equally to all sectors of society
- **Epidemiology:** the study of how often diseases occur in different groups of people and why
- **Diseases of poverty:** Diseases associated with places/people of poverty
- **Diseases of affluence:** Diseases associated with places/people of affluence
- **Alternative of indicators of food and health:** Because food and health are not easy to measure
- Will consider interactions between food and health (i.e. gender, TNCs, national governments)
- Considers alternative ways of assessing agricultural sustainability alongside possibilities for improving food supplies and global health

**Daily per capita calorie supply:** The total calorie supply available for human consumption divided by the total number of actual population utilizing it.

**Food security index:** Index based on 68 factors for benchmarking level of food security
- Includes Political stability risk, Sufficiency of supply, Proportion of population under the global poverty line and Food consumption
**Global hunger index:** Index to measure hunger at different levels and its effects over time
- Based on:
	- Undernourishment
	- Child stunting
	- Child wasting 
	- Child mortality

**Malnutrition:** Imbalance in nutrients received from food (undernutrition, obesity, diet-related noncommunicable diseases)
- 4 types:
	- **Wasting:** Having a low weight for your height
		- Indicates recent, severe weight loss
		- Associated with higher risk of death
	- **Stunting:** Having a low height for your age
		- Prevents physical/cognitive development
	- **Underweight** 
	- **Micronutrient deficiencies**
