# R1.3 Energy from fuels

**Combustion:** chemical reaction of a substance with oxygen to produce carbon dioxide and water.
- ==Very exothermic== -> releases a lot of energy
- **For metals:** 
	- Combines with oxygen -> **metallic oxide** 
		- Produces lots of heat and light energy
	- Less reactive metals -> rusting and tarnishing 
- **For non-metals:** 
	- Combines with oxygen -> **non-metallic oxide** 
	- **NO FIXED RATIO**
		- Can produce different compounds 
			- $P_4(s) + 5O_2(g) → P_4O_{10}(s)$ 
			- $P_4(s) + 3O_2(g) → P_4O_6(s)$            
- **For [[Organic compounds explained|organic compounds]]:** 
	- **Complete:** 
		- Produces carbon dioxide and water very easily
		- Flammable (gas>liquid>solid)
			- Blue flames
		- I.e. $CH_3CH_2OH_{(l)} + 3O_{2(g)} → 2CO_{2(g)} + 3H_2O_{(g)} \qquad ∆\text{H is negative}$ 
	- **Incomplete:** 
		- **Somewhat limited oxygen:**
			- $CO_{(g)} + H_2O_{(l)}$ 
		- **Severely limited oxygen:**
			- $C_{(s)}+H_2O_{(l)}$ 
		- Happens when too fast, low temp, not enough oxygen
			- Orange/red flames
